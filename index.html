How To Play

SPIT

Get Ready!!

This game can ruin friendships, and you may lose a finger.

You may be thinking, "isn’t this a card game?”

Yes. Yes it is.

Objective

Get rid of all of your cards and land your opponent with the whole deck.

Play as many rounds as necessary to make this happen.

Set Up

One card at a time, create five stacks with increasing numbers. The left most stack should have one card, the right most stack should have five cards.

Flip over top card in each stack.

From here, like numbered cards can be moved to their match, as long as there are always five stacks. / ex. the 2 of Hearts and 2 of Clubs can be stacked, allowing you to flip over an additional card on your bare pile.

Your opponent will have the same setup.

Put excess cards between players in an individual draw pile to each player’s right. This will be used throughout the game

Game Play

Counting “one, two, three: Spit,” each player flips over the top card from their draw pile, thus creating two center piles.

Players can then play cards from their own set-up into either of the center piles.

Each card played should be one number or face value up or down from the top card of the center pile.

Both players should have five stacks in their set-ups at all times, until they have less than five cards. Cards can be rearranged to achieve this.

If neither player is able to use their set-up cards on the center piles, count again and flip a new card from each players’ draw pile onto their center pile.

Once a player has played all of their cards, both players can attempt to slap the smallest center pile.

Whichever pile a player slaps first will be absorbed into their deck.

Use all of your collected cards to create a new set-up and play again.

When numbers get low...

Once one player does not have enough remaining cards to complete their set-up, they will play the round with less cards.

If a player does not have enough cards for a draw pile, the other player will flip their top draw pile card for the only center pile.

When the round comes to a close, slap the table where the second center pile would have been.

The game is over when a player gets rid of all of their cards, and is able to slap the table.

The winning player will have no draw pile, no set-up, and no center pile to take in.

Play Responsibly.
